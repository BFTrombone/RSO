During the 2005-2006 season Rainier Symphony is celebrating 25 years of bringing South King County audiences great classical music close to home. One of the finest community orchestras on the west coast, Rainier Symphony reaches over 5,000 people a year with a broad range of music from the classical and pops repertoire.

Our diverse membership includes teachers, physicians, engineers and students who share a love of music and their community. Under the direction of Music Director David Wayne Waltman, the orchestra regularly performs with internationally recognized artists. This season's guests will include Roman Svirlov, concertmaster of the Moscow State Symphony Orchestra, pianist Denice Dameron, and The Bottom Line Duo.

Thanks to our friends from the City of Tukwila Arts Commission, the Tukwila School District, the City of Kent, the Renton School District and local businesses and patrons, we look forward to many more years of providing our community with great music close to home!
