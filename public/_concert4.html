Enigma

Saturday, April 18 - 7:30 pm
Sunday, April 19 - 3:00 pm

Foster Performing Arts Center
4242 South 144th Street, Tukwila

Our final classics concert of the season will focus on transformation, and will feature the music of Shostakovich, Higdon, and Elgar. Each of these composers have pieces that play with the idea of making something new out of something old.

Our April concert addresses the idea of disguise and beguile. Each of the pieces on this program portrays some element of transformation. Shostakovich’s October, Op. 131 acts as a commentary on the Russian Revolution of 1917, where the Bolshevik’s took power. In this piece, we get a sense of before and after the revolution. Following that, the orchestra will feature Dr. Erik Steighner on Jennifer Higdon’s Concerto for Soprano Saxophone. In relation to the concert theme, this piece is disguised as a saxophone concerto, though it was initially an oboe concerto, then adapted onto the saxophone.

Following intermission, the orchestra will play Edward Elgar’s beautifully transformative composition: Variations on an Original Theme, Op. 36, commonly referred to as the Enigma Variations. This piece is 14 separate variations on one melody, which Elgar used as an opportunity to create an homage to multiple people in his circle. His ability to enchant the listener with new ideas on the same melody is equal parts playful, thoughtful, emotional, and exhilarating.

Bring those from your own circle with you and join us for a weekend of musical depiction and deception.

Shostakovich October, Op. 131
Higdon Concerto for Soprano Saxophone and Orchestra
    Eric Steighner, Saxophone
Elgar Variations on an Original Theme (Enigma), Op. 36
