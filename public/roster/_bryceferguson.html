Bryce Ferguson, Bass Trombone

Return to Roster

Bryce Ferguson has been active in the Seattle Area music scene for over 40 years, initially as a percussionist, moving to tuba and finally trombone during high school. While attending the University of Washington, he studied trombone with Stuart Dempster and Dennis Smith, former principal trombone of the Los Angeles Philharmonic. He was part of the UW Symphony Orchestra that travelled to Washington DC to perform at Kennedy Center. He was also a member of the Seattle Youth Symphony Orchestra during this time.

Since his university days, he has performed with most of the community orchestras in Seattle, most notably Seattle Philharmonic, Bellevue Philharmonic, Broadway Symphony (now Orchestra Seattle) and Philharmonia Northwest. He was with the Federal Way Philharmonic in the Seattle Opera House for their performance of Beethoven's 9th Symphony in conjunction with the 1990 Goodwill Games held in Seattle. He has also been a guest performer with orchestras such as Everett Philharmonic and Lake Union Civic Orchestra. He joined Rainier Symphony as Bass Trombone during the 2008 - 2009 season, which included a memorable performance of Mahler's 2nd Symphony, "Resurrection".

When not performing, he can be found during the day busily creating software solutions for major businesses throughout the area. He enjoys sailing and spending time with family.
