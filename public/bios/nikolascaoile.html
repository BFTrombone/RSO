Nikolas Caoile, Guest Conductor

Dr. Nikolas Caoile is Director of Orchestras at Central Washington University. There, he has grown the orchestras into the preeminent collegiate orchestra programs of the Northwest. The CWU Symphony Orchestra has consistently won invitations to perform at the Washington Music Educators Association Conferences. A passionate believer in music education for all ages, Caoile has conducted numerous educational and community engagement concerts. Heis also a featured presenter for the Seattle Symphony's Talk Music series. In October 2009, Caoile made his New York City debut conducting the Philharmonic Orchestra of the Americas in collaboration with Morphoses/The Wheeldon Company. Caoile is also a popular guest conductor and clinician with youth orchestras, collegiate ensembles, andhigh school honor orchestras. In November 2010, Caoile directed the Alaska All-State Orchestra. In the February 2011, Caoile will guest conduct the Rainier Symphony Orchestra. This summer, he will join the Icicle Creek Music Center as director of the Icicle Creek Summer Symphony. Next year, he has beeninvited to guest conduct the Salem Chamber Orchestra. Also an accomplished pianist, Caoile is a frequent recitalist performing throughout Washington State and the Northwest. He has performed with the Third Angle New Music Ensemble. He is also a faculty member of the Kairos Chamber Music Lyceum.

In June 2010, Caoile was appointed Music Director and Conductor of the Wenatchee Valley Symphony Orchestra. He has also appeared with several professional, youth, and collegiate orchestras. He has guest conducted the Yakima Symphony Orchestra, Gig Harbor Symphony, Olympia Symphony Orchestra, and Salem Chamber Orchestra. Caoile has conducted internationally at the Royal Conservatory of Music in Toronto, the Institut Musical de Provence Aubagne in France. He has also competed at the Vahktang Jordania Competition and the 9th Cadaqués Conducting Competition.

Nikolas Caoile is a native of Portland, OR and now resides in Ellensburg, WA with his wife, mezzo-soprano, Melissa Schiel.
