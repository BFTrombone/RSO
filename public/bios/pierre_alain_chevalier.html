Pierre-Alain Chevalier


French-born conductor Pierre-Alain Chevalier is a passionate performer and music educator. He has taught and conducted primary, secondary and university students as well as community musicians and professionals. He has performed professionally as a vocalist and has conducted opera, musical theater productions, choirs and orchestras.

In concert Chevalier has conducted the Bayou City Symphony, Rose City Chamber Orchestra, Liedertafel Harmonie (choir of the German-American Society of Portland), Hartt School Symphony Orchestra, Contemporary Players and Musical Theater, Moores School Symphony, Chamber and Concerto Orchestras, Cecelia Chamber Choir and Willamette University's wind ensemble, choirs and Dramatic Vocal Arts ensemble. As a music teacher he has led students in performances at Community Christian School, Fowler Middle School, Sam Houston High School, Klein Forest High School, and worked as an orchestra clinician for the past several years in the Cypress-Fairbanks, Katy, Klein, Spring and Houston independent school districts.

Currently Chevalier is the Artistic Director and Founder of the Bayou City Symphony, Music Director of Houston Community College's Southwest Choir, and Director of Choral Activities at Sam Houston High School.

Chevalier holds degrees from the Moores School of Music (DMA: Orchestral Conducting 2014), The Hartt School (MM: Orchestral Conducting 2010) and Willamette University (BM: Music Education 2006). Chevalier began his musical studies at the age of five in Portland, Oregon, where he spent most of his childhood.
