Susan Carroll

Susan Carroll was born in Tacoma and is an alumni of both the Tacoma and Seattle Youth Symphonies. She left the Northwest after high school to continue her musical studies in Philadelphia.

She attended Temple University as a student of Mason Jones (former solo horn, the Philadelphia Orchestra), and completed her education at The Curtis Institute of Music where she was a student of Myron Bloom (former solo horn, the Cleveland Orchestra).

While at Curtis, Ms Carroll performed under the baton of numerous renowned conductors including Leonard Bernstein and Serge Celebidache. With three of her Curtis colleagues she won a solo competition which resulted in a solo appearance with the Philadelphia Orchestra performing the Symphonia Concertante in E flat for four solo winds and orchestra.
