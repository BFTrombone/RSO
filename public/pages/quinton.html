Quinton I. Morris

This year, Mr. Morris has enjoyed a busy schedule balancing careers as a concert violinist, chamber musician, teacher, director and founder of The Young Eight String Octet. Originally from Renton, Washington, he has performed solo and chamber music performances in the United States, the Middle East, and Europe. He is the recipient of numerous competition awards including The Boston Conservatory Chamber Music Honors Competition, the Louisiana Junior Philharmonic Orchestra Young Artist's Concerto Competition, Seattle Philharmonic Concerto Competition, The NAACP

ACT-SO Competition, the North Carolina Federation of Music Clubs String Competition and the Ebony Showcase Music Award.

Mr. Morris has appeared as a concerto soloist with his alma mater youth symphony orchestras, Renton and Seattle Youth Symphonies at Carco Theater and Benaroya Hall in Seattle, Washington. His solo and chamber music concerts have included performances at the Boston Public Library Recital Series, Seattle Art Museum, Watson and Crawford Halls at the North Carolina School of the Arts, Harvard University's Saunders Theatre and the Eastman Theatre in Rochester, New York. Mr. Morris has opened for Cyndi James Gossett, Lou Rawls, Ben Vereen, and Keith Washington. He has collaborated with Metropolitan Opera singers Indra Thomas and Angela Brown and members from the Providence String Quartet. He also appeared with mezzo-soprano Nancy Fabiola Gerrera, soprano Yolanda Auyanet and David Antony Lofton in recital in Las Palmas, Spain in December 2001. He frequently collaborates with pianist David Lofton and harpist Lisa Michelle Beckley in duo recitals.

As the director and founder of The Young Eight, he has performed with the string octet in recitals across the country. The Young Eight, a group of distinguished African American string players from the most elite and prestigious music schools and conservatories are in residence each summer at the North Carolina School of the Arts. In addition, The Young Eight have conducted residency programs through the Harlem School of the Arts, Winston-Salem State University, The Juilliard School, Bloomingdale School of Music and First Philadelphia Charter School for Literacy.

Administratively, Mr. Morris was the youngest member sworn on as vice-chair for the King County Children and Family Commission. He served as secretary on the Children and Family Commission Executive Board, and as a member of The Youth Involvement Executive Board and Xavier University Executive Student Body Association Board. He has also served as administrative assistant to The Boston Conservatory String Department chairman and Boston Pops member Andrew Mark. In addition, Mr. Morris was recognized during Black History Month by the State of Washington's House of Representatives for his accomplishments as both a concert organizer and musician. As the founder and director of The Young Eight, Mr. Morris' responsibilities include marketing, artistic directing, fundraising and promoting for all concert events.

During the summer of 2002, Mr. Morris served as a teacher's assistant to Walter Schwede, (former Seattle Symphony associate concertmaster) at Seattle Youth Symphony's Marrowstone Music Festival. Mr. Morris has also taught at the Boston Arts Academy High School.

Mr. Morris is currently a candidate for the Doctor of Musical Arts at The University of Texas at Austin studying violin with Daniel Ching, first violinist of the Miró String Quartet and concert violinist Brian Lewis. Mr. Morris is a high graduate of Renton High School. He began his studies at Xavier University of Louisiana, where he studied pre-law and violin with Rachel Jordan. He received a Bachelor of Music degree from The North Carolina School of the Arts where he studied violin with Kevin Lawrence and chamber music with Nicholas Mann. He also holds a Master of Music degree from The Boston Conservatory, where he studied violin with renowned violinist Lynn Chang and chamber music with Boston Trio member Irina Muresanu and Lydian String Quartet member Judith Eisenberg.
