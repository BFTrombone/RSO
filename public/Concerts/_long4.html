Our April concert addresses the idea of disguise and beguile, and each of the pieces on this program portrays some element of transformation. Shostakovich’s October, Op. 131 acts as a commentary on the Russian Revolution of 1917, when the Bolshevik’s took power. In this piece, we get a sense of before and after the revolution, and the tumult in between. Following that, the orchestra will feature Dr. Erik Steighner on Jennifer Higdon’s Concerto for Soprano Saxophone. In relation to the concert theme, this piece was initially an oboe concerto, and has been adapted brilliantly for the saxophone.

Following intermission, the orchestra will play Edward Elgar’s beautifully transformative composition: Variations on an Original Theme, Op. 36, commonly referred to as the Enigma Variations. This piece has 14 variations on a single melody, which Elgar used as an opportunity to create an homage to people in his circle. His ability to enchant the listener with new ideas on the same melody is equal parts playful, thoughtful, emotional, and exhilarating.

Bring those from your own circle with you and join us for a weekend of musical depiction and deception.
